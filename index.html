01  ·  How It Works

The Concept

Three couples. Each couple drafts 4 real Top Chef contestants — 2 per partner. Every episode, your chefs earn or lose points based on what actually happens on screen. Both partners' scores pool together into one couple total, and the couple with the most points at the end of the season wins. One of your chefs will eventually hear "pack your knives and go." Try to make sure it's someone else's.

There's also an individual leaderboard tracking each partner's personal score from their own 2 chefs — so you're playing with your partner and against them simultaneously.

✍️

Draft Day

Snake draft. Each couple picks 4 chefs total — Partners A and B each choose 2. 12 of the 13 available chefs get drafted; one sits out all season.

📺

Watch Every Episode

Points are earned each episode based on Quickfire and Elimination results. The commissioner tracks scores and posts the leaderboard.

📊

Pool Your Points

All 4 of your couple's chefs score every week. Both leaderboards update — the couples standings and the individual player rankings.

🏆

Win the Season

The couple with the highest combined total at the finale wins. Consistency beats flash — a team whose chefs regularly finish top-3 will dominate.

The two-competition twist There are two competitions happening at once. The Couples Leaderboard (all 4 chefs' scores pooled) is the main event. The Individual Leaderboard ranks each partner by their own 2 chefs only — bragging rights inside your couple.
02  ·  Scoring System

Every Point Counts — This is Top Chef. Not Top Scallops.

Points are tracked per chef, per episode. A well-rounded roster beats a gamble on one big name. Points stack — a chef who wins the Quickfire and the Elimination in the same episode is having a very good week for your couple.

Consistency wins — even Gail could tell you that, if she had an original thought.

03  ·  Draft Format

Draft Format — Pack Your Knives if You Pick Wrong

Snake draft. Couple order is drawn randomly before draft day. Within each couple, you and your partner decide who picks first. Each pick has a 90-second clock. Your time starts... NOW. If you're still debating at 80 seconds, you're already flaming out like Jen Carroll in Season 8.

The pool: 12 chefs available (the 2 eliminated in Episodes 1–2 are out). All 12 get drafted across 2 rounds — 2 picks per person, 4 per couple. No chef goes undrafted.

Draft Order — Random Individual Draw

All 6 players draw individually from a bowl — no couple gets a structural advantage. Whatever order comes out is the snake order for all 4 rounds. The example below shows one possible result; yours will be determined on draft day.

04  ·  Roster Structure

Your Four Chefs

Each couple fields 4 chefs — 2 belong to each partner. All 4 score every week they appear. When one of your chefs gets eliminated, their owner scores with 1 chef for the rest of the season. No replacements, no trades between couples. You drafted them, you ride with them. Pack your regrets instead. And whatever you do — do not let your chef make risotto.

05  ·  Rules & Edge Cases

The Fine Print

These rules have more structure than Gail's feedback. The commissioner has final say on anything not listed here — think of them as Tom, but with less flannel. And if the commissioner's own chef is in a disputed call, another couple co-arbitrates that dispute only.

T

Ties at Season End

If two couples end with identical total points, the tiebreaker goes in order: (1) most total Elimination Challenge wins across all 4 chefs, (2) most Top-3 finishes, (3) coin flip on a video call with all 3 couples present.
✕

Chef Quits or Can't Continue

If a chef leaves for any reason other than elimination (medical, personal, production issue), their owner gets no negative points. That chef scores 0 going forward; their partner plays out the season with their remaining chef.
2

Double Elimination Episodes

Both chefs score −8. If both eliminated chefs belong to the same couple, that's a brutal week — both partners now each have 1 chef left. Two eliminations in one week. Tom doesn't enjoy it either. (He does.)
↩

Last Chance Kitchen Return

When a chef re-enters the main competition from LCK, they automatically re-activate on their original couple's roster. The returning chef earns the +4 return bonus and resumes normal scoring from that episode forward.
