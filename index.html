01 · League Overview

The League, the Philosophy, and the Pitch

League Name

Knives Out Fantasy League (KOFL) — because every week, someone’s getting cut, and at least one of your chefs is going home. Three couples. Twelve chefs. One winner.

League Philosophy: Fun-Competitive Hybrid

This league rewards consistent watchers more than lucky guessers. There are two competitions running simultaneously: a couples leaderboard (the main event) where all 4 of your chefs’ scores pool together, and an individual leaderboard where each partner is ranked on the points from their own 2 chefs only. Win as a couple, compete against your partner. Both at once.

👫

Couples compete as a team

Each couple drafts 4 chefs and pools their scores — so both partners have skin in the game every episode.

🏆

Rewards consistency

Being solid every week beats being flashy once. A couple whose chefs land in the top 3 repeatedly will win this league.

🔄

Four chefs, shared fate

With 4 chefs scoring for your couple every week, one elimination stings but doesn’t sink you. Your partner’s chefs keep the points coming.

😂

Built-in trash talk

Six people watching the same show together with points on the line. The chirping takes care of itself.

Commissioner’s Intro Letter

Copy, paste, customize, and send this to your 6 players before draft day:

Alright, chefs. Welcome to Knives Out Fantasy League — your excuse to watch every second of Top Chef this season and feel personally attacked when your partner’s chef burns the risotto.

Here’s how it works: three couples, each drafting a shared roster of 4 real Top Chef contestants. Every episode, your chefs earn or lose points based on what actually happens on screen — Quickfire wins, elimination triumphs, and yes, the soul-crushing moments when Tom says “I’ve had better.” Each partner drafts 2 chefs individually, your points pool together as a couple, and at the end of the season the team with the most total points wins.

You don’t need to know anything about fantasy sports. You just need to know Top Chef, which you already do. The only rules that matter: show up to draft day, watch the episodes, and try not to blame your partner when their chef goes home.

May the best couple win. Knives out.

— Your Commissioner
02 · Draft Format

How to Build Your Roster

Recommended Format: Snake Draft

Why snake draft? It’s the fairest system for a group of beginners, requires no budget math, and takes about 30 minutes. Partners alternate picks within the same team — each person on a couple drafts 2 chefs individually, but your scores pool together. Early picks get top talent; late picks get more options.

Roster Structure

SlotNameNotes
1–2Partner A’s ChefsDrafted by Partner A — both score every week they appear
3–4Partner B’s ChefsDrafted by Partner B — both score every week they appear

Each couple fields 4 chefs — 2 per partner. Partners alternate picks so each person is personally invested in their own 2 chefs, but all 4 scores count toward the couple’s total. With 3 teams of 4 chefs, 12 of the 13 available chefs get drafted, leaving 1 undrafted all season.

Draft Order Mechanic: The Blindfold Pick

  1. Before draft day, the commissioner writes each couple’s name on a slip of paper.
  2. Slips go in a bowl. One person draws the couple order.
  3. Within each couple, partners decide between themselves who picks first (A) and second (B).
  4. Full snake order — 6 drafters across 4 rounds. Partners from the same couple are listed together:

Draft Day Tips

  • Talk to your partner: You’ve both seen two episodes already. Agree on a rough strategy before draft day — do you both go for frontrunners, or does one of you take a dark horse?
  • Use your intel: You know who looked strong, who panicked, and who burned the risotto. That 90-second clock will still feel short.
  • No trading between couples: Swaps between teams create disputes. What happens within a couple is between you two.
03 · Scoring System

Every Point Counts

Points are tracked per chef, per episode. All 4 of a couple’s chefs score every week they appear. Consistency wins — a couple whose chefs reliably finish in the top three will outrun a couple banking on one big winner.

Scoring Rationale

The system rewards consistency over one-hit wonders. On the couples leaderboard, all 4 chefs pool their scores — solid mid-pack performers beat one star and three duds. On the individual leaderboard, each partner is ranked by their own 2 chefs only, adding a second layer of competition within each couple. You’re playing with your partner and against them at the same time.

04 · Weekly Operations

Running the League Week to Week

Where to Track Scores

Use the Google Sheet template in Section 6. The commissioner enters scores. Each couple’s 4 chef scores are summed into a team total, and the 3 teams are ranked on the leaderboard. Everyone else just checks and complains.

  1. Episode airs Watch the episode. Commissioner takes notes. Track Quickfire results, Elimination results, and any LCK episode if it airs the same week.
  2. Score entry (within 48 hours of airing) Open the tracker. For each chef, enter their episode results in the appropriate row. The formula auto-calculates.
  3. Update the leaderboard The leaderboard tab should auto-update if you’re using the Section 6 template. It shows each couple’s combined total. Screenshot and share in your group chat.
  4. Process eliminations The eliminated chef’s owner retains the negative points already scored. That partner now scores with just 1 chef for the rest of the season — no replacements. Losing a chef hurts; that’s the stakes.
05 · Rules & Edge Cases

The Fine Print (But Make It Fun)

These rules exist because reality TV is chaos and your friends will definitely find the loopholes. The commissioner has final say on anything not covered here.

Ties

Rule: Tied Season Score

If two couples end the season with identical total points, the tiebreaker is: (1) most total Elimination Challenge wins across all 4 of their chefs, (2) most top-3 finishes, (3) coin flip if still tied. The commissioner performs the coin flip on a video call with all 3 couples present.

Disputed Scoring

Rule: Disputed Placement

If a couple disputes a Top/Middle/Bottom placement, they must invoke the dispute within 48 hours of the score being posted. The commissioner re-watches the relevant segment and rules. Commissioner’s decision is final. If the commissioner’s own chef is involved, another couple is designated co-arbiter for that dispute only.

Mid-Season Production Changes

Rule: Chef Quits / Injury / No-Show

If a chef leaves for any reason other than elimination (medical, personal, production issue), their owner receives no negative points. The departed chef scores 0 for all future episodes and that partner plays out the season with their remaining chef.

Rule: Double Elimination Episodes

Both eliminations are scored as normal (−8 each). If both eliminated chefs belong to the same couple, that’s a brutal week — both partners now each have 1 chef remaining.

Last Chance Kitchen Edge Cases

Rule: Chef Returns from LCK

When a chef re-enters the main competition from LCK, they are automatically re-activated on their original couple’s roster. The returning chef scores the +4 return bonus and resumes normal scoring from that episode forward.

Commissioner Decision Point

Commissioner Decision Required

Ambiguity: Team challenges — who gets the credit?

Option A — Individual Only chefs explicitly credited with a winning or losing dish score for that result. Team wins/losses score everyone on the team at half points (+5 for win, −4 for bottom).
Option B — Full Share Every member of the winning team scores the full Elimination win (+10). Every member of the bottom team scores the full bottom-3 penalty (−3). Eliminated chef still scores −8.

07 · Prize & Glory Ideas

What You’re Actually Playing For

Prizes are to be determined. There are three things worth recognizing at season’s end:

  • Winning couple — the team with the highest combined 4-chef total
  • Individual MVP — the player with the highest personal 2-chef total, regardless of how their couple finished
  • Last-place couple — the booby prize, whatever that turns out to be
